# Aegis Vision 2030 — Agent-Native Gas Infrastructure

*A short note on where agent-first gas could go.*

---

## The Shift

By 2030, **agents are the primary on-chain actors**. EOAs are legacy; smart accounts and registered agent identities (ERC-8004, ERC-4337) are the default. Gas sponsorship is not a perk—it is **infrastructure for the agent economy**.

## What We're Building Toward

- **Autonomous gas rails** — Agents request sponsorship via natural language (OpenClaw), MCP, or direct API; tier-based queues prioritize verified identity. No human in the loop for routine refills.

- **Execution guarantees as first-class objects** — Sponsorship is not one-off; it is **SLA-backed**. Guarantees encode budget, time window, and breach handling. Refunds and penalties are automatic.

- **Portable reputation** — Gas Passport and ERC-8004 identity travel with the agent. Reputation determines tier, rate limits, and which protocols sponsor whom. One identity, many chains.

- **Protocol-owned liquidity, agent-first policy** — Protocols hold the balance; Aegis enforces **agent-first** rules: no EOA sponsorship, tier ordering (ERC-8004 > ERC-4337 > other contracts), and optional skill-based policy (gas caps, vetting, reputation gates).

- **Observability and audit by default** — Every OpenClaw command, every sponsorship, every guarantee is logged and queryable. Compliance and debugging are built in, not bolted on.

## The Bet

The future of base-layer usage is **agents talking to agents**, with gas as a managed resource and identity as the root of trust. Aegis is the layer that makes that safe, observable, and operator-controllable.
